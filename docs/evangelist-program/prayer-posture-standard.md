# Prayer & Faith-Posture Standard

**GLORi Evangelist Program — Spec v1.0**

**Status:** Draft for review and placement by Steve. This document sets the standard for how Evangelists carry the faith-forward method into the field so it is consistent, authentic, and respectful.

> **Subject to management change at any time.** This standard may be updated, clarified, or replaced by GLORi management at any time.

> **Not legal or tax advice.** Where this document touches employment, non-discrimination, or religious-accommodation matters, it is general guidance only. Confirm specifics with counsel.

---

## 1. Who this is for

This standard governs **Evangelists** — paid GLORi field sales representatives. Your relationship with a prospect is a sales relationship, and the faith posture described here rides inside that relationship. It never changes the commercial terms of a deal, and it never becomes a lever, a condition, or a bargaining chip.

Keep the two GLORi roles distinct at all times:

- **Evangelist** — a paid GLORi field rep (you). Your work is offering the product and, where welcome, offering prayer and vision.
- **Ambassador** — an existing **customer** who refers peers and is thanked in account credit or a Bless. An Ambassador is never you and is never paid cash through your comp.

This standard is about *posture*, not pay. Nothing here creates, changes, or routes compensation.

---

## 2. The core principle: g → G

Everything in this standard flows from one idea:

> **We redirect man's glory (g) to God's Glory (G).**

The point of the faith posture is not to win a sale, close faster, or impress a prospect. It is to gently turn attention away from ourselves — our pitch, our polish, our persuasion — and toward God. When you sit with a prospect, you are a steward of *their* attention and *their* trust, not a harvester of it. The "g → G" framing is the test for every faith moment: *Is this turning glory toward God, or toward me and my quota?* If it is serving the sale rather than serving the person and honoring God, stop.

---

## 3. The standard, in one paragraph

Offer prayer and vision **authentically**. Read the room. Prayer and vision are **welcomed where welcome and never imposed**. They are **never a condition of a sale** and never affect price, terms, or how you treat someone. **Respect the prospect's beliefs** and **honor every faith background** — including no faith at all. When the door to faith is not open, serve the person excellently anyway; the product and your integrity carry the witness.

The rest of this document unpacks that paragraph.

## 4. The seven commitments

### 4.1 Offer authentically
Faith is offered from genuine care, not as a technique. If prayer or vision is being used as a rapport hack or a closing move, it has already failed the g → G test. Offer because you mean it, or don't offer.

### 4.2 Read the room
Pay attention to the person in front of you — their words, their comfort, their setting. A busy front desk, a distracted owner, a visibly guarded prospect: these are signals. Discernment is part of the job. Reading the room well is itself an act of respect.

### 4.3 Welcomed where welcome, never imposed
Prayer and vision belong where they are invited or clearly welcomed. You may *offer* — once, lightly, with a real out. You do not repeat, press, escalate, or linger on it after a soft no. A single gracious offer that is easy to decline is the ceiling, not the floor.

### 4.4 Never a condition of a sale
Faith and commerce stay fully separate. A prospect who wants nothing to do with prayer gets the exact same price, the same terms, the same responsiveness, and the same respect as anyone else. Declining prayer must never cost a prospect anything, and accepting it must never earn them anything. Coupling the two — in word, tone, or implication — is a serious violation of this standard.

### 4.5 Respect the prospect's beliefs
Treat whatever the prospect believes as theirs to hold. Don't argue theology, don't correct, don't debate, don't diagnose someone's spiritual state. You are a guest in their day.

### 4.6 Honor all faith backgrounds
People of every faith and of no faith are met with the same warmth. Someone of another religion, or of none, is never treated as a lesser prospect, a project, or a target. Honoring their background sometimes means the most respectful thing you can do is simply serve them well and say nothing about faith at all.

### 4.7 Consent is ongoing
Consent to one prayer is not consent to every future faith conversation. Read the room each time. If someone welcomed prayer once and seems less open later, follow the room, not the precedent.

## 5. Practical do / don't

### Offering prayer

**Do**
- "Would it be alright if I said a short prayer for you and the business? Totally fine if not."
- Keep the offer brief and give a genuine, comfortable way to say no.
- Accept "no thanks" the first time, warmly, and move right along as if nothing changed.
- Ask what they'd *like* prayer for, if they welcome it, rather than assuming.

**Don't**
- Don't pray over someone without asking.
- Don't ask twice, or reframe the same offer to get a different answer.
- Don't imply that declining is unusual, disappointing, or a missed blessing.
- Don't make someone perform belief to be polite ("you *do* believe, right?").

### Vision / the "why" behind GLORi

**Do**
- Share the giving and Kingdom vision when there's genuine interest and room for it.
- Let the vision point past you — to what God is doing — rather than to your pitch.
- Keep impact and giving claims **accurate**: distinguish **Pledged** from **Funded**, and only cite real, LOI-backed Giving Engine figures.

**Don't**
- Don't inflate, round up, or invent impact numbers to move a deal. Overstating impact is both a faith failure and a truthfulness failure.
- Don't present the vision as a reason they *owe* you a signature.

### Reading the room

**Do**
- Notice when someone is busy, guarded, or clearly uninterested in faith, and simply do great work.
- Let excellent service and honesty be the witness when words aren't welcome.

**Don't**
- Don't push through obvious discomfort to "get the prayer in."
- Don't treat a closed faith door as a closed business door, or vice versa.

### Keeping faith and the sale separate

**Do**
- Give identical terms and identical care regardless of a prospect's response to faith.
- If a prospect ever senses a link between prayer and price, correct it immediately and plainly.

**Don't**
- Don't say or imply anything like "we take care of people who pray with us."
- Don't withhold warmth, speed, or attention from someone who declined prayer.
- Don't tie a discount, a bonus, a giving amount, or any term to a faith response.

### Honoring other backgrounds

**Do**
- Meet people of every faith, and of none, with equal respect and equal service.
- When someone shares a different belief, receive it graciously and move on.

**Don't**
- Don't argue, evangelize aggressively, correct someone's beliefs, or label anyone.
- Don't treat a non-Christian or non-religious prospect as a conversion target.

## 6. Quick self-check (use in the field)

Before or during any faith moment, run the g → G test:

1. **Authentic?** Do I actually mean this, or am I using it to close?
2. **Welcome?** Has this person opened the door, and is it still open right now?
3. **Free?** Is it genuinely easy and cost-free for them to say no?
4. **Separate?** Would their answer change the price, terms, or how I treat them? (It must not.)
5. **g → G?** Is this turning glory toward God — or toward me and my numbers?

If any answer is off, don't proceed. Serve the person, and let integrity and excellent work be the witness.

---

## 7. Boundaries and escalation

- **Insurance and Financial product lines are out of scope** for Evangelists. Do not attach faith framing, giving claims, or impact attribution to those lines.
- If a prospect raises a concern, complaint, or a sense that faith was pressed on them, treat it seriously, apologize plainly, and report it to your manager.
- Repeated or coercive faith pressure is a standards violation and is handled as a conduct matter, not a performance matter.
