# Marketing51 Sales Commission Plan — Model D

**GLORi Evangelist Program · Field Sales Compensation**

*Draft for review. Replaces the prior "v3 (No Draw)" plan document. This plan is subject to management change at any time (see §11).*

---

## 1. Purpose

This document defines how a GLORi **Evangelist** — a paid GLORi field sales representative — earns cash compensation for acquiring and retaining GLORi customer accounts. It is the single authoritative statement of the Evangelist commission structure and supersedes all prior versions.

An Evangelist is paid in **cash commission and cash residual**. This is distinct from the **Ambassador** program: an Ambassador is an existing GLORi *customer* who refers peers and is rewarded in account credit or a Bless — never cash. The two programs never mix. An Evangelist's pay never flows through Ambassador rewards. (Where a deal happens to arrive through a customer referral, a separate $250 ambassador deduction may apply to that deal's economics; that deduction is not, and never becomes, Evangelist pay.)

This plan covers the standard monthly-subscription business. Enterprise (Quetrex) deals are compensated separately — see §9. Insurance and Financial product lines are out of scope for Evangelists and are excluded from this plan entirely.

---

## 2. Plan at a glance

- **20% of monthly recurring revenue (MRR)** on each account you sell, for that account's **months 1–12**.
- **5% residual on MRR** for that account's **months 13–60** (years 2–5), in any year you qualify (see §6).
- Both the 20% and the 5% are **paid only while you are employed** as an Evangelist. There is **no base salary and no draw**.
- **Stacking one-time milestone bonuses** up to **$13,500** in your first year (see §7).
- **No clawbacks.** Commission and residual already paid are yours to keep.

By design, an account's full year-1 commission and its four-year residual tail come out **equal** (20% × 12 months = 5% × 48 months). This makes the plan easy to explain: what you earn acquiring an account, you can earn again by keeping it.

## 3. Plans and tiers

GLORi customers subscribe on one of three monthly plans. Your commission is a percentage of that plan's MRR.

| Tier | Monthly MRR |
|---|---|
| **Starter** | $100 |
| **Growth** | $400 |
| **Pro** | $1,000 |

---

## 4. Commission structure

### 4.1 Acquisition commission — 20%, months 1–12

For each account you sell, you earn **20% of that account's MRR every month for its first 12 months**, while you are employed.

| Tier | Monthly (20% × MRR) | Year 1 (× 12) |
|---|---|---|
| Starter | $20 | **$240** |
| Growth | $80 | **$960** |
| Pro | $200 | **$2,400** |

### 4.2 Residual — 5%, months 13–60

Once an account passes its first 12 months, it can pay you a **5% residual on MRR for months 13 through 60** — years 2 through 5 — for a total residual life of four years per account. Residual is **employed-only** and **subject to the annual qualification gate in §6**.

| Tier | Monthly (5% × MRR) | Per residual year (× 12) | 4-year residual tail |
|---|---|---|---|
| Starter | $5 | **$60** | $240 |
| Growth | $20 | **$240** | $960 |
| Pro | $50 | **$600** | $2,400 |

### 4.3 Five-year per-account lifetime

Each account carries a hard **5-year (60-month) commission cap**. After month 60, an account pays no further commission or residual, regardless of how long it stays a customer.

| Tier | Year 1 (20%) | Residual yrs 2–5 (5%) | **5-yr lifetime / account** |
|---|---|---|---|
| Starter | $240 | $240 | **$480** |
| Growth | $960 | $960 | **$1,920** |
| Pro | $2,400 | $2,400 | **$4,800** |

---

## 5. Billing basis

- Commission and residual are calculated on **actual MRR billed** for each account, **each month**.
- The **first month is prorated** to the account's live/activation date.
- If an account changes plan tier, its MRR — and therefore your commission — adjusts to the new tier from the effective date forward.
- If an account cancels, commission on that account simply stops with the last month billed. Amounts already paid are not reversed (see §8).

---

## 6. Residual qualification gate

The 5% residual rewards active, producing reps. It is gated annually:

- You **earn the 5% residual in a given year only if you produced at least 50 new accounts in that year** — roughly one new account per week.
- The gate is measured on a **calendar/rolling-year basis and re-qualifies every year.** Each year stands on its own.
- A year in which you produce **fewer than 50 new accounts earns no new residual for that year.** You still earn full 20% acquisition commission on every account you sell — the gate only affects the 5% residual.
- **Residual already paid in a prior qualifying year is never clawed back.** A short year affects only that year's residual; it never reaches backward.

The 5-year per-account cap (§4.3) continues to bound how long any single account can pay residual, independent of the gate.

*Note: 100 accounts per year remains the stretch target and the year-milestone bonus threshold (§7); 50 is the residual qualification bar, not the target.*

---

## 7. Milestone bonuses (stacking)

One-time cash bonuses reward fast, high-volume production. They **stack** — hitting all three pays the full combined amount.

| Milestone | Target | Bonus |
|---|---|---|
| Fast start | 10 new sites in month 1 | **$1,000** |
| Quarter | 25 new sites in 3 months | **$2,500** |
| Year | 100 new sites in 12 months | **$10,000** |
| **All three (cumulative)** | | **$13,500** |

Each bonus is paid once, on first achievement.

---

## 8. Employment and no-clawback rules

- **Employed-only:** both the 20% commission and the 5% residual are paid only for periods during which you are an employed Evangelist. When you leave, future commission and residual stop.
- **No clawback of paid amounts:** commission and residual already paid to you are yours. Leaving does not reverse them, and an under-50 year (§6) does not reverse residual already earned in a prior qualifying year.
- **No base, no draw:** this is pure commission. A configurable draw feature exists in the software but is **defaulted OFF** and is not part of this plan.
- **No leader override and no senior/elevated commission tier** exist under Model D.

---

## 9. Enterprise (Quetrex) accounts

Enterprise deals sold under the **Quetrex** banner are **bespoke** and are **not** compensated on the monthly Starter/Growth/Pro table above. Enterprise compensation is negotiated per engagement and documented separately. Nothing in §§3–7 applies to enterprise accounts.

## 10. Worked economics — illustrative

The example below is a **fully-ramped upper bound**: it assumes 100 accounts, all live for a full year, a 25 / 50 / 25 mix across Starter / Growth / Pro, and full 5-year retention with the residual gate met every year. A real first year ramps in over the year and lands lower; this is an illustration of the mechanics, not a forecast.

| Component | Calculation | Amount |
|---|---|---|
| Acquisition commission (20%, yr 1) | 25×$240 + 50×$960 + 25×$2,400 | **$114,000** |
| Residual per year (5%) | 25×$60 + 50×$240 + 25×$600 | **$28,500 / yr** |
| Residual, years 2–5 | $28,500 × 4 | **$114,000** |
| Stacking milestone bonuses | Fast start + Quarter + Year | **$13,500** |
| **5-year total on this cohort** | | **≈ $241,500** |

Because a rep typically builds a *rolling* book, each new year layers fresh 20% acquisition income on top of an accumulating 5% residual stack from prior qualifying years. Under the §6 gate, a year with fewer than 50 new accounts still earns full acquisition commission but adds no residual for that year.
