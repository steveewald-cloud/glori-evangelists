## Model D at a glance

The Evangelist plan is a **pure-commission** field-sales model for **paid GLORi reps**
(cash commission + residual). Two components, both **employed-only** — pay stops the
day a rep leaves, and there is **no clawback** of anything already paid:

| Component | Rate | Window | Lever it pulls |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Acquisition commission** | **20% of MRR** | Account months **1–12** | — |
| **Residual** | **5% of MRR** | Account months **13–60** (years 2–5) | **Retention** |
| **Stacking milestone bonuses** | $1,000 / $2,500 / $10,000 | Year 1 | **Volume** |

**Symmetry that makes it easy to explain:** year-1 pay equals the four-year residual
tail per account (20% × 12 months = 5% × 48 months).

**Residual qualification gate:** a rep earns the 5% residual in a given year **only if
they produced ≥ 50 new accounts that year** (~1 per week). It is a **calendar/rolling-year
gate, re-qualified annually** — a year under 50 new accounts earns **no new residual that
year**, but residual already paid in prior qualifying years is never clawed back. A
**5-year per-account cap** bounds how long any single account can pay residual.

Tiers (monthly MRR): **Starter $100 · Growth $400 · Pro $1,000.**

## Per-account cost (retained account, rep employed & residual-qualified)

| | Starter ($100) | Growth ($400) | Pro ($1,000) |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Year 1** (20% × MRR × 12) | $240 | $960 | $2,400 |
| **Residual / year** (5% × MRR × 12) | $60 | $240 | $600 |
| **Residual, years 2–5** (× 4) | $240 | $960 | $2,400 |
| **5-year lifetime / account** | **$480** | **$1,920** | **$4,800** |

**Cost as a share of revenue:** across a retained account's full 5-year life, total
Evangelist comp is a flat **~8% of collected MRR** for every tier (e.g. Growth: $1,920
comp on $24,000 of 5-year MRR). It is **front-loaded** — most of it lands in year 1.

## Headline: the 100-site rep, 5-year picture

Fully-ramped upper bound — a **25 / 50 / 25** mix (Starter / Growth / Pro), all 100
accounts live a full year, 5-year retention, rep employed and residual-qualified
throughout. Blended average account = **$475 MRR**.

| Line | Amount |
|---|---|
| Acquisition-year commission (20%) — 25×$240 + 50×$960 + 25×$2,400 | **$114,000** |
| Each residual year (5%) — 25×$60 + 50×$240 + 25×$600 = $28,500 × 4 yrs | **$114,000** |
| Stacked milestone bonuses (Year 1) | **$13,500** |
| **5-year total on that cohort** | **≈ $241,500** |

This is **one illustrative fully-ramped cohort**, not a ramped year-1 P&L — a real
year-1 ramps in and lands lower. A rep with a *rolling* book earns fresh 20% acquisition
income each year **plus** an accumulating 5% residual stack from prior qualifying years.

## The levers leadership controls

- **Bonuses = the volume lever.** The stacking $1,000 / $2,500 / $10,000 ladder rewards
  fast, high-count production in year 1. Capped, one-time, per rep.
- **Residual = the retention lever.** 5% for years 2–5 rewards keeping accounts alive
  and keeping the rep on the team — it pays only while employed.
- **The 50/yr gate ties residual to an active, producing rep.** Residual is not an
  annuity for past work; a rep must re-clear ≥ 50 new accounts each year to earn new
  residual that year. This is the primary dial for how much residual liability the
  company carries.
- **"Subject to management change at any time."** The entire plan is explicitly
  adjustable — retained as a stated condition in the Drive doc, the `/join` page, and the
  rep acknowledgment.

---

## Company-cost implications of the residual tail

- **The tail is real but bounded four ways:** (1) **employed-only** — it evaporates when
  a rep leaves; (2) the **50-accounts/year gate**, re-qualified annually, so a slowing rep
  stops accruing new residual; (3) the **5-year per-account cap**; (4) **no clawbacks**, so
  liability is always backward-settled and never a receivable.
- **Steady-state magnitude:** for a rep who keeps qualifying and retaining, residual
  compounds — up to **~$28,500/yr** on a fully-retained 100-site cohort — and roughly
  **doubles** the lifetime cost of a retained account versus year-1 alone. Net effect
  still lands near **~8% of the revenue that account produces** over five years.
- **This is a retention cost, not a giveaway.** Residual dollars are only owed on MRR the
  company is actually still collecting, from a rep still on the team who is still hitting
  volume. If any of those stop, the residual stops with them.
